1 — The nap window: explained in plain English
The concept is shown once on first use, then referenced naturally. The word "window" is replaced everywhere with language that communicates consequence.
Why timing matters 🌙
Babies can only stay awake so long before becoming overtired — when that happens, stress hormones make them harder to settle and sleep worse, not better.
There's a sweet spot — a window when she's tired enough to drift off easily. Miss it and you're fighting cortisol, not tiredness.
